Karnataka MBBS Counselling 2023: Counselling Process


Karnataka MBBS Counselling 2023 is a state Centralized Online Counselling Process. The sole authority to conduct online MBBS/BDS Counselling 2023 is Karnataka Examinations Authority i.e. KEA Bangalore.


Candidates need to visit the official website at https://kea.kar.nic.in for registration based on NEET-UG Score 2023. Candidates can apply to MBBS/BDS Courses in various Government/Private medical colleges in the state of Karnataka.


To secure an MBBS seat in deemed medical college, Candidates will have to register at https://mcc.nic.in. Four rounds of counselling take place followed by a Stray Vacancy round of NEET Counselling 2023 is subject to the availability of MBBS/BDS Seats after the mop-up round.


Admissions to the Medical/Dental/Ayush colleges of Karnataka are solely based on the NEET-UG 2023 score. Only NEET 2023 qualified candidates can participate in the Karnataka MBBS Counselling 2023. Details information is being shared in the article below.

Registration:


  • Candidates have to register on the website i.e. https://kea.kar.nic.in  and submit the registration fee which is Rs. 500/2500/5500 as per the category in which they are applying, through Internet banking or any online mode.


  • KEA Bangalore will publish the Karnataka NEET-UG 2023 merit list for the qualified candidate as per the secured marks and required percentile. Selected candidates will be allowed to participate in further events of Counselling.

Choice Filling:


  • Candidates need to fill in Online choices at the https://kea.kar.nic.in website by using their registration number and transaction id generated at the time of registration.


  • Candidates have to fill in their choices of colleges as per their priority. They can fill choices as many as they wish.

Allotment Result:


  • Candidates can check the seat allotment status from the KEA Bangalore at https://kea.kar.nic.in. 


  • Candidates are advised to download their allotment letter which needs to be produced at the time of reporting to the allotted college.


  • Seats are allotted to the candidates based on rank, scored marks, and the choices of their preferences.

Reporting to Allotted College:


  • This is the final event when the candidates need to report to the allotted college with an allotment letter before the last date of admission.


  • Seats will be considered as confirmed, once the fee payment and verification of documents will be taken place.

Karnataka MBBS Counselling 2023: Eligibility Criteria


  • Candidates must have qualified NEET-UG 2023.

  • Candidates must have the domicile of Karnataka (Differ for NRI/Other Quota)

  • Candidates must have passed 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English as Core subjects with a minimum of 50% marks, 40% marks are required for SC/ST Category candidates.

  • The candidates should have completed 17 years of age as of Dec 31st, 2023. Candidates born on or after Jan 2nd, 2005, will not be entertained.

  Karnataka MBBS Counselling 2023: Seat Category


Karnataka MBBS Counselling 2023 is going on. The last date to register online for MBBS/BDS Counselling is July 21st, 2023 at 6 pm. In the state of Karnataka, there are several categories of seats, in which admission is provided by KEA Bangalore. Each category carries a separate reservation and admission process. 


Candidates need to go through the article once before applying for Karnataka MBBS Counselling 2023. There are 4 types of seats in the entire Medical/Dental/Ayush Colleges in the state of Karnataka, that can be claimed as per the eligibility mentioned by KEA Bangalore.

G- Government Seats


It stands for the seats in Government Medical Colleges and Government Shares in Private Medical/Dental Colleges in Karnataka.   


  • 100% of seats in Gov. Medical/Dental Colleges

  • 40% of seats in Medical Private Non-Minority Colleges

  • 35% of seats in Dental Private Non-Minority Colleges

  • 25% of seats in Medical Private Minority (Religious/Linguistics) Colleges

  • 25% of seats in Dental Private Minority (Religious/Linguistics) Colleges

P- Private Seats


It refers to the Medical/Dental seats in the Private Colleges which came under the ambit of KPCF, AMPCK, and KRLMPCA. These terms are explained below.


  • 40% of seats in Medical Colleges are reserved as Private quota seats in Karnataka Non-Minority Colleges.

  • 50% of seats (Out of 40%) are only for Karnataka Candidates.

  • The remaining 50% of seats (Out of 40%) are Open quota for NEET-UG 2023 All India Merit.

N- NRI Seats


It stands for 15% of the total seats reserved for NRI Quota in different Minority, Non-Minority, and Professional Private Medical Colleges in Karnataka.

Q- Other Seats


It means 5% of the total seats are reserved for others, which refers to the candidate, outside Karnataka in KPCF, KRLMPCA, and AMPCK Colleges.
